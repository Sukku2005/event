Pongal story

Pongal is a harvest festival celebrated in South India to give thanks for the year's produce and to mark the end of the farming season: Celebrating the harvest: Farmers give thanks for the healthy crop, while others thank the farmers for producing the crops.

Thanking the sun god: Hindus believe that the sun god moves around the Earth in his chariot, and the festival symbolizes this. Thanking the rain god: The festival also thanks Lord Indra, the god of rain. Thanking farm animals: The festival gives thanks to the farm animals that help with the harvest.
Celebrating the change of seasons: Pongal marks the beginning of the warmer months and the northward journey of the sun. Marking the beginning of Thai: Pongal also marks the beginning of the Tamil month of Thai, which is considered an auspicious month.
